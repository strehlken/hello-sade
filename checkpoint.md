## What it is

A Mother's Day card for Sade from me and the kids (Harrison 8, Scarlett
6, Sloane 4), framed as a 19-slide click-through "alien field report"
investigating whether Sade is the family's leader. The kids' real
testimony serves as evidence, the family photos as exhibits. Closes
with the aliens learning the earthling custom: *"I love you."*

## Design system

- **Typography:** Source Serif 4 (display + body), JetBrains Mono (HUD
  + labels), Caveat (signature). Fraunces was used briefly and
  rejected for "quirky stems."
- **Palette:** cream `#f4efe5` paper, deep navy `#0e1417`/`#15212a`
  cosmic, alien teal `#4a6e73`, scarlet `#c5523b` accent. Per-kid
  voice colors: Sloane plum, Harrison gold, Scarlett scarlet, Alien
  teal.
- **Layouts:** magazine spreads (50/50 photo + text); triptychs and
  diptychs for multi-photo moments; pause slides for typography
  breathers.
- **Photo cropping:** per-image `object-position` in CSS, with a
  calibration mode that existed briefly then was removed in favor of
  hand-edits in the source.

## Hard-won lessons

- **Centering bug.** Spent multiple turns fighting cells that hugged
  the left edge of multi-photo slides. Root cause: `aspect-ratio` +
  `max-height` shrinks grid cells *narrower* than their `1fr` tracks
  on shorter viewports; with default `justify-self: stretch` they
  anchor to the start of the track. Misdiagnosed at first as a
  min-content blowout (since one image was a much higher resolution).
  Correct fix: `grid-template-columns: auto auto` + `justify-content:
  center` on the strip. Saved as a memory under
  `feedback_centering_pattern.md`.
- **Saga override.** A leftover `max-width: 1180px` on the saga strip
  was silently stripping the `calc(100% - 48px)` safety from the base
  rule, breaking centering on narrow viewports. Removed.
- **Per-image positioning > calibration UI.** Built a drag-to-position
  calibration mode (writes to localStorage, exports CSS), used it for
  one pass to lock in 7 photo crops, then ripped it out for
  simplicity. The per-photo CSS rules at the bottom of the styles
  block are the single source of truth.

## Open Graph

A 1200×630 PNG (`og-image.png`) generated by Python/PIL with the
cover aesthetic — dark gradient, stars, "Hello, Sade." in Georgia,
italic subtitle "We are aliens from the distant future. We come in
peace." Wired up via `og:*` and `twitter:*` meta tags.
